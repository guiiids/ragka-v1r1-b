Replace the column with a zero-dead-volume union and Restriction Capillary.3. Put the autosampler in mainpass mode.4. Set the 2-position/6-port MCT valve to forward-flush mode (valve position 1-2).5. Bypass the degasser by connecting all four solvent lines directly to the solvent selection valve to avoid damage during passivation [1], [2].### Passivation Solution Preparation:1. Dilute concentrated (65%) nitric acid with an equal volume of HPLC-grade water to create a solution containing 32.5% nitric acid [1], [2].### Cleaning Procedure:1. Place inline filters for all channels into a clean beaker containing HPLC-grade isopropanol.2. Set pump flow rates as follows: - Purge at 5 mL/min for 5 minutes (25% per channel). - Run isopropanol through each channel at 0.5 mL/min for 10 minutes.3. Repeat steps using HPLC-grade water instead of isopropanol.### Passivation Process:1. Use the prepared Passivation Solution and pump it through each channel at a flow rate of 0.25 mL/min for at least 4 hours but no more than 8 hours [2].2. Flush thoroughly with fresh HPLC-grade water until pH >4.5 is achieved in effluent from PEEK tubing outlet [3], [4].### Final Steps:1. Reconnect degasser channels and solvent selection valve after confirming neutralized pH (>4) of LC effluent.2. Replace restriction capillary and union with your analytical column.3. Replace frits for outlet filters if necessary [3], [4].Caution: Ensure thorough flushing after acidic solvents are used, as residual acidity can impact negative ionization performance during subsequent analyses [1].
